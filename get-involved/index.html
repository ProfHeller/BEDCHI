Get Involved

Join BEDCHI

BEDCHI welcomes students, volunteers, and professionals interested in digital cultural heritage documentation, community-engaged research, and Indigenous collaboration. Whether you're looking to gain hands-on field experience, develop technical skills, or contribute to meaningful heritage preservation work, there are opportunities to get involved.

Field School Opportunities

BEDCHI hosts field schools and workshop-based projects in the Bears Ears region, providing intensive training in digital documentation methods alongside community-engaged research practices.

What You'll Learn

  • Photogrammetry techniques (handheld and drone-based 3D capture)
  • 360° panoramic documentation
  • 3D model processing and optimization
  • Real-time rendering and immersive visualization
  • Ethical protocols for working with Indigenous communities
  • Collaborative research methodologies

Who Should Apply

  • Undergraduate and graduate students in anthropology, archaeology, digital humanities, museum studies, or related fields
  • Professionals seeking to expand their digital heritage skills
  • Anyone committed to respectful, community-centered cultural heritage work

No prior technical experience required! BEDCHI field schools are designed to teach reproducible methods from the ground up, emphasizing collaborative learning and hands-on practice.

Current Field School Program

BEDCHI partners with the Institute for Field Research (IFR) to offer a field school in digital archaeology and cultural heritage documentation in the Bears Ears region. This intensive program provides hands-on training in 3D documentation, geospatial technologies, and community-engaged research practices.

Volunteer Opportunities

We welcome volunteers to assist with various aspects of the project, from field documentation to post-processing and digital asset management.

Ways to Contribute

  • Field Documentation: Participate in on-site recording sessions in the Bears Ears region
  • Data Processing: Help process and optimize 3D models for archiving and visualization
  • Metadata Management: Assist with cataloging and organizing digital assets
  • Outreach & Education: Support educational programs and public engagement activities
  • Web Development: Contribute to digital platforms and interactive experiences

Volunteer Benefits

  • Hands-on experience with cutting-edge digital heritage technology
  • Training in ethical community-engaged research practices
  • Networking with professionals in archaeology, anthropology, and digital humanities
  • Contribution to meaningful Indigenous cultural heritage preservation
  • Potential co-authorship opportunities on publications and presentations

Research Collaborations

BEDCHI supports collaborative research projects that advance digital heritage methodologies, Indigenous interpretive authority, and community-engaged documentation practices. We welcome proposals from researchers, institutions, and tribal organizations interested in partnership.

Areas of Collaboration

  • Digital documentation workflows and technical development
  • Indigenous knowledge systems and interpretive frameworks
  • Immersive visualization and virtual reality applications
  • Long-term digital preservation and archival strategies
  • Educational programming and public outreach
