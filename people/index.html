Our Team

Eric Heller
Lecturer, University of Southern California

Eric Heller is an anthropologist at USC who has been deploying and teaching photogrammetry and immersive digital tools for cultural heritage work since 2012. He leads BEDCHI's field documentation and visualization efforts, bringing together 3D capture, VR prototyping, and community-engaged research to support education, stewardship, and Indigenous-centered interpretation.

Benjamin (Ben) Bellorado
Assistant Curator of Archaeology, Arizona State Museum

Benjamin (Ben) Bellorado is the Assistant Curator of Archaeology at the Arizona State Museum (University of Arizona). His work focuses on Indigenous cultures of the northern Southwest, with long-term experience in research and collaboration that bridges archaeological evidence, material culture, and community-engaged heritage interpretation.

Noah Pleshet
Associate Professor of Anthropology, University of New Brunswick

Noah Pleshet is an Associate Professor of Anthropology at the University of New Brunswick and an applied sociocultural and environmental anthropologist. He brings expertise in community-engaged research, cultural landscapes, and resource management, helping BEDCHI integrate documentation workflows with interpretive goals and student learning.

About BEDCHI

The Bears Ears Digital Cultural Heritage Initiative documents and interprets cultural heritage sites and landscapes through 3D scanning, immersive visualization, and community-engaged research in partnership with Indigenous communities, researchers, and land managers.
