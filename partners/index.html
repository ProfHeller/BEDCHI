Our Partners

Universities

University of Southern California (USC)

The University of Southern California brings expertise in digital humanities and advanced visualization technologies. Their faculty and students contribute to developing innovative methods for cultural heritage documentation and public engagement through digital media.

University of New Brunswick

The University of New Brunswick provides specialized knowledge in geospatial analysis and archaeological survey techniques. Their research focuses on landscape archaeology and the integration of traditional ecological knowledge with modern scientific methods.

University of Arizona

The University of Arizona contributes cutting-edge research in Southwestern archaeology and anthropology. Their expertise in ceramic analysis, chronology, and regional cultural patterns helps contextualize artifacts within broader historical frameworks.

Institute for Field Research

The Institute for Field Research (IFR) connects students and early-career researchers with field opportunities in archaeology and cultural heritage. Through IFR, BEDCHI engages students in hands-on training in digital documentation methods, fostering the next generation of cultural heritage professionals while advancing collaborative research goals.

Tribal Partners

Navajo Nation Heritage and Historic Preservation Department

The Navajo Nation Heritage and Historic Preservation Department ensures that cultural materials are documented and presented in ways that honor Navajo traditions and perspectives. Their guidance is essential to maintaining the cultural integrity and appropriate stewardship of items with ancestral connections to Navajo people.

Zuni Cultural Resources Advisory Team

The Zuni Cultural Resources Advisory Team provides critical consultation on cultural protocols and traditional knowledge related to artifacts and sites within the Bears Ears region. Their participation ensures respectful treatment of culturally sensitive materials and proper recognition of Zuni cultural heritage.

Museums

Natural History Museum of Los Angeles County (NHMLAC)

The Natural History Museum of Los Angeles County is a premier institution dedicated to inspiring wonder, discovery, and responsibility for the natural and cultural worlds. Through their partnership with BEDCHI, they provide expertise in collections management, conservation, and public education, along with world-class facilities and research staff.

Edge of the Cedars State Park Museum

Edge of the Cedars State Park Museum, located in Blanding, Utah, houses one of the premier collections of Ancestral Puebloan artifacts. Their proximity to the Bears Ears region and deep relationships with local communities make them an invaluable partner in contextualizing and interpreting cultural materials.

Arizona State Museum

Arizona State Museum at the University of Arizona maintains extensive collections from the Southwest and provides crucial expertise in archaeological conservation and curation. Their research programs advance understanding of the region's cultural history and support ethical stewardship of cultural heritage.

A:shiwi A:wan Museum and Heritage Center

The A:shiwi A:wan Museum and Heritage Center on the Zuni Pueblo serves as the cultural repository for the Zuni people. This tribally-operated museum ensures that Zuni cultural heritage is preserved and interpreted from a Zuni perspective, maintaining cultural sovereignty over ancestral materials.

Conservation Groups

Canyonlands Natural History Association

The Canyonlands Natural History Association is a nonprofit organization dedicated to inspiring appreciation and stewardship of public lands in southeastern Utah. Through educational programs, publications, and community engagement, they support conservation efforts and connect people with the cultural and natural heritage of the Bears Ears region and surrounding landscapes.

Federal Agencies

U.S. Forest Service (USFS)

The U.S. Forest Service manages portions of the Manti-La Sal National Forest adjacent to Bears Ears National Monument. Their archaeologists and cultural resource specialists work to document, protect, and interpret cultural sites on federal lands, collaborating with tribal nations and researchers to ensure proper stewardship.

Bureau of Land Management (BLM)

The Bureau of Land Management administers significant portions of the Bears Ears National Monument and surrounding public lands. BLM cultural resource programs support archaeological research, site protection, and collaborative management with tribal partners, providing critical data and access for digital documentation initiatives.
